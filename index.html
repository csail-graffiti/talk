What is social media?

Social data in Graffiti is composed of independent objects You can only change your own objects --- moderation, etc. are simple interpretations of the data.
The issue with such a system is context.

Design Philosophy

Make no assumptions.

Design Overview

  • Social data is composed of independent objects (posts, likes, relationships, etc.) which can be referred to by unique identifiers.
  • Users can only modify their own objects; moderation and collaboration are interpretations of data.
  • Data objects lack an intrinsic schema; norms evolve naturally as a folksonomy.
  • Data objects have no extrinsic organization; collections of objects are built from queries.
  • Contextual boundaries are an intrinsic property of the data.

Context

When we communicate we form mental models of our audience:

  • What are these people interested in?
  • What conversational norms do they expect?
  • ...

We shape what we say or don't say based on these mental models ðŸ“– .

Context

We build our mental models from identity cues and context cues. Online, a context might be defined:

  • By app: "on twitter", "on facebook"
  • By category: "in a channel", "on a forum", "at #something"
  • In relation other things: "in its comments section", "on my profile"
  • In a hierarchy: "in a thread", "in a directory"
  • In relation to a physical space: "within a radius"

A context helps us form a mental model of the people around us: our imagined audience. When people don't have a context they experience context collapse, many stop engaging.
By imposing some extrinsic strucure (e.g. a directory structure) you can find counter examples
