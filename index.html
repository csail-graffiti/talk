Context

In real life things happen in physical places. These places may be public and even contain similar people, by space itself. When I'm at a bar, I implicitly know the people around me also made a choice to come to the bar so they've made an implicit so they must be expecting the type of activity associated with bar. Even if these places are public, we act differently they can carry different meaning. Church and Bar. Online things Suppose I'm talking to people under the tag "Paddy's Pub" which is associated with the query { type: "post", content: { "$type": "string" }, tags: "Paddy's Pub" } Conversation that goes like: Now my colleague come's around and look "My Wall" e.g. all the posts by me { type: "Post", _by: [My ID] } content: "yoooooooooooo let's do shots" tags: ["Paddy's Pub"] Some recruiter come's "at school", "at work". Even if places are public, they carry different meaning. Online things happen in places too. By app: "on twitter", "on facebook" By category: "in a channel", "in a forum", "on a subreddit", "on gay TikTok" In relation to other places: "in a comments section", "in a thread" "in a folder" By identity: "on my wall", "@me" By location: "at an event", "in my neighborhood", "in a radius" Sometimes things happen in multiple places at once, sometime things are in intersecting places. In a system where you can just query for whatever you want, how can I make sure?

Context is often a crucial but invisible design element of a social space.
