What can we do about lock-in?

Socialist Approach

Effect change through collective action.

  • Write petitions or legislation that will change existing platforms.
  • Create an entirely new platform and convince everyone to switch.

Slow and require sustained collective effort1 :
often solving problems in the rear-view mirror.

Graffiti

  • An infrastructure that (arguably) any social application can be built on top of.
  • Applications that have overlapping functionality naturally interoperate.

*Clarification: Graffiti does not integrate existing platforms, it is a prototype of an entirely new ecosystem.

Anarchist Approach

Effect change independently or through voluntary collaboration.

  • Use, design or customize whatever interfaces, filters, algorithms, moderators and broadcast patterns you want!
  • Many designs can be explored and iterated on in parallel. Fast evolution!

How does this all work?

Our Design

  • Social data is composed of independent objects (posts, likes, relationships, etc.) which can be referred to by unique identifiers.
  • Users can only modify their own objects; moderation and collaboration are interpretations of data.
  • Data objects lack an intrinsic schema; norms evolve naturally as a folksonomy. (Like ActivityStreams).
  • Data objects have no extrinsic organization; collections of objects are built from queries.
  • Contextual boundaries are an intrinsic property of the data.

Moderation as Interpretation

  • Subscribe to moderators just like you subscribe to posts!
  • You could give moderation power to friends, community leaders or public options.
  • Maybe you'll use a system like PolicyKit!

Context and Collapse

When we communicate we form mental models of our audience:

  • What are these people interested in?
  • What conversational norms do they expect?

We shape what we say based on these models
and we form our models from context cues.

Context

... an abstract place and the identities, situations and norms associated with that place.

  • App: on twitter, on facebook
  • Category: in a channel, in a subreddit, at #graffiti
  • Person: on my profile
  • Content: in its comments section
  • Hierarchy: in a thread, in a directory
  • Physical space: within a radius
  • ...

But what is a place when data can show up in many queries?

Context Collapse!

When people experience context collapse they can either overly censor themselves or end up annoying and being censored by other people1.

This is already a problem!

By collapsing everything onto one infrastructure it could get a lot worse!

Wrapping Up

Why is this titled Graffiti?

Related Work

  • Solid: makes your own data portable but does not break the network effect.
  • ActivityPub: great for communication with specific people or with people on your server. Not great for overlapping or spontaneous communities.
  • Web3: something isn't necessarily better just because it's decentralized.

Open Questions

  • Would this be a haven for hate groups? Or would the diverse and overlapping "views" dampen echo chambers?
  • If left to their own devices would people just make social media even more addictive/engaging?
  • Would people remove all healthy friction? Is friction a hack we don't need anymore?
  • Is it possible to decentralize? Would a democratically run non-profit suffice?
